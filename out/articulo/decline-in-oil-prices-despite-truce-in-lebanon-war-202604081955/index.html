Decline in Oil Prices Despite Truce in Lebanon War, but Strait of Hormuz Remains a Concern

The price of oil has decreased despite the onset of the global pandemic due to COVID-19, resulting from a recent truce in the Lebanon war. However, the Strait of Hormuz remains a zone of significant concern for international petroleum interests.

Expected drops in supply due to the war and diplomatic negotiations have caused market imbalances, leading to a sudden drop in prices. Yet, Iran's blockade of traffic in the Strait of Hormuz continues to pose an important risk to global petroleum security.
